Metal Gear Solid (re release)

This is a review of metal Gear solid, the game that was originally released in 1998. It's been 26 years since this game came out and even right now I am enjoying this game tremendously. Feelings of nostalgia aside, I am having so much fun playing this game. The cinematography, the cutscenes, the depth of the story.... even the gameplay feels fresh in 2024. It is phenomenal and I enjoy it even more so because I'm older now and I can appreciate this game more. The depth of the story to understand it requires a certain type of maturity. Just to give you an idea of how long its been, when the game was first released I was 18 years old. That's how long ago this was. It almost feels like a different life time. Hideo Kojima the maker of this game is a genius. He and his vision was way, way ahead of its time. For some strange reason I don't know what it is, but I've played the sequels to this game some directed by him. all of them actually directed by him but it does not even come close to the sense of adventure and fun gameplay wise that I had with the first game the second game was great but it didn't invoke the same feelings as the first one.

In case you have been living on a different planet or you weren't even born when this game first came out, this video game is about Solid Snake(it's just his name), a soldier who infiltrates a nuclear weapons facility to stop a terrorist threat from FOXHOUND(which is an evil special forces unit).Solid Snake must free the hostages and stop the terrorists from launching a nuclear strike.

The graphics and cinematography were way ahead of its time. The presentation was top notch. When other games that came out at the time the voice acting in the games were cheesy and sounded like a b-grade film. With this game, the voice acting was like something you would find in a blockbuster hollywood movie. In fact, the whole game feels like a AAA blockbuster film. The soundtrack, the background music score, the sound editing was arguably according to Hollywood standards. I remember feeling a lot of emotion that few movies could conjure up.

The gameplay included elements of stealth which debuted in the previous games in the series but were very limited in scope since everything was two dimensional in the previous games. The 3d games allowed for much more that could be done in this regard. As such a lot of games that came after it have tried to implement stealth mechanics with varying degrees of success. In addition to That there were rpg elements, you had to manage health, heal yourself etc etc. The game also included some of the most memorable boss fights I had encountered up to that time. Even today, the many different kinds of approaches you could take to pacify a boss were astonishing at that time. To conclude, this is one of if not my most favourite game of all time. For some reason, despite the advancement in graphics and sound technology, the overall experience for me, just doesn't get any better than this.
